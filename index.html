Geometric Audit: Precision vs. Interference

We verified the algorithm's integrity by transitioning from a theoretical baseline to production-scale deployment:

  • Baseline (N=1): Unlearning a single user is a perfect mathematical projection along the feature space with \(R^2 = 1.0\).
  • Production (N=20): Scaling to batches reveals Batch Interference, where competing gradients dilute precision to \(R^2 \approx 0.16\).

Despite variance, Cosine Similarity remained locked at 0.427339, proving zero geometric drift.

Mathematical Foundation

\[ w_{t+1} = w_t + \eta \nabla \mathcal{L}_{forget}(w_t) \]

By applying Projected Gradient Ascent (PGA), we maximize error exclusively on a targeted forget-batch while preserving the decision boundaries of the retain-set.

Solving the Stopping Point Paradox

We discovered a 34-step Safety Window where the model maintains peak utility.

Monitoring the Validation Loss on the Retain Set (our "Blind Metric") allows systems to autonomously halt unlearning at Step 31 before catastrophic forgetting begins.

The Memorization Trap

An MIA conducted at Step 31 revealed target confidence only dropped from 99.09% to 98.93%.

Highly confident financial profiles are stuck on the "Sigmoid Plateau," where standard gradient ascent is insufficient for outlier erasure without destroying model integrity.
